Compare and Contrast

Let $S = \mathbb{Q}[x,y,u,v]$ and $M = \mathbb{Q}[x, y, u, v]/(x, y^2, u^4, v^8)$. Then the "simplicial" decomposition of $\beta(M)$ is given by

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \beta(M) &= 168 \pi(0,1,3,7,15) + 60 \pi(0,2,3,7,15) + 210 \pi(0,2,5,7,15) \\ &+ 30 \pi(0,4,5,7,15) + 60 \pi(0,4,6,7,15) + 240 \pi(0,4,6,11,15) \\ &+ 240 \pi(0,4,9,11,15) + 60 \pi(0,8,9,11,15) + 30 \pi(0,8,10,11,15) \\ &+ 210 \pi(0,8,10,13,15) + 60 \pi(0,8,12,13,15) + 168 \pi(0,8,12,14,15). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

In contrast, the "non-simplicial" decomposition of $M$ is given by

\begin{equation*} \beta(M) = (1 \cdot 2 \cdot 4 \cdot 8) \sum_{\sigma\in \mathrm{Perm}(\{1,2,4,8\})}\pi(0,\sigma(1),\sigma(1)+\sigma(2),\sigma(1)+\sigma(2)+\sigma(4),15). \end{equation*}
